3. CORE COMMITMENTS
--------------------
Every team member agrees to the following without exception:

- Attend all scheduled team meetings, or give at least 24 hours' notice
  if unable to attend.
- Complete assigned tasks before the agreed deadline.
- Communicate openly in the group channel — silence is not acceptable.
- Commit code or submit work to GitHub regularly; at minimum once per
  working day during a sprint.
- Review and give feedback on teammates' work when asked.
- Raise blockers immediately — do not wait until the deadline to say
  something is not done.


4. MEETINGS & COMMUNICATION
-----------------------------
- The Scrum Master (Wasim) will schedule standups at least twice per week
  during active sprints.
- All members must respond to messages in the group chat within 24 hours
  on weekdays.
- If a member misses two consecutive standups without prior notice, it is
  considered non-contribution.
- Decisions made in meetings are binding for all members, including those
  who were absent but notified.


5. TASK ALLOCATION & TASKBOARD
--------------------------------
- Tasks will be assigned at the beginning of each sprint and tracked on
  the shared taskboard (GitHub Projects).
- Each member is responsible for updating the status of their tasks
  (To Do / In Progress / Done).
- No member may claim credit for work they did not complete.
- If a task is too large or unclear, the member must say so immediately —
  not at the deadline.


6. CONSEQUENCES FOR NON-CONTRIBUTION
--------------------------------------
The following escalation process will be applied strictly and without exception:

Stage 1 — Warning
  Trigger: Task missed or 2 standups missed without notice.
  Action:  Scrum Master sends a written warning via the group chat
           and logs the incident.

Stage 2 — Formal Notice
  Trigger: Second missed task or no response within 48 hours.
  Action:  The team notifies the module leader via email, copying all
           members, with documented evidence.

Stage 3 — Escalation
  Trigger: Continued inaction after formal notice.
  Action:  Full escalation to module leader with request to record the
           member as non-contributing. The team submits without their work.

NOTE: Personal circumstances (illness, family emergencies) are understood
and will always be considered — provided the member communicates in advance.
Silence is never acceptable.


7. GITHUB & VERSION CONTROL
-----------------------------
- All code must be committed to: https://github.com/wasim-khans/salonEase
- No one pushes directly to main — use feature branches and pull requests.
- Commit messages must be clear and descriptive.
- Pull requests must be reviewed by at least one other team member
  before merging.


8. RESPECT & FAIRNESS
-----------------------
- All members treat each other with respect, regardless of background
  or skill level.
- Feedback is given constructively — on the work, not the person.
- No member takes over another's work without discussion.
- Credit is distributed fairly and honestly in all submissions.


9. AGREEMENT
-------------
By committing this file, each team member confirms they have read,
understood, and agree to abide by this Code of Conduct for the remainder
of the SalonEase project.
